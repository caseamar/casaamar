feat(knowledge): introduce registry-driven Knowledge Platform and control center

Summary
- Added knowledge-registry.json as the central catalogue for knowledge, policy, intent, source, inbox and test datasets.
- Changed _worker.js to discover and load knowledge datasets from the registry instead of hardcoded source paths.
- Added /api/status with dataset health, entry counts, test counts, inbox counts and load errors.
- Added knowledge-center.html as a simple read-only control center for platform health and maintenance overview.
- Added knowledge-entry.schema.json as the canonical schema for future facts, notes, guides and recommendations.
- Added knowledge-inbox.json for unreviewed notes, keywords and links that must not influence AI answers yet.
- Added KNOWLEDGE-WORKFLOW.md describing public content, internal knowledge, drafts and external sources.
- Moved the development version badge to the left so it is not hidden by the chat launcher.

Files changed
- index.html
- _worker.js

Files added
- knowledge-registry.json
- knowledge-entry.schema.json
- knowledge-inbox.json
- knowledge-center.html
- KNOWLEDGE-WORKFLOW.md

Why
Casa Amar needs a knowledge architecture that can grow without turning the worker into a collection of hardcoded integrations. The registry makes datasets discoverable and auditable, while the inbox and schema create a safe workflow for both structured facts and informal owner notes.
